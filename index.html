Moana Waialiki

Moana is an independent traveler who loves the ocean. Despite her occasional bouts of self-doubt, she is fiercely proud of her identity and fearlessly tackles new challenges. When faced with adversity, she remains steadfast in her pursuit of her beliefs and gives her all to new endeavors.

Image Description
Merida Dunbroch

Merida defies the traditional princess stereotype by being an independent, fearless, courageous, defiant, and obstinate young woman. She excels in archery and loves nothing more than spending time on her horse Angus, two of her many athletic pursuits. As the Brave featurette "Cutting the Class" shows, she is also obviously good with a blade. Because of her excessive tomboyishness, Merida often acts in an ungraceful manner and has terrible manners.

Hua Mulan

In the beginning, Mulan is portrayed as an independent misfit. A clumsy girl, she needs to learn how to follow protocol. Regardless, she is kind-hearted and wants to be true to herself while protecting her family's reputation. However, this is challenging for her to achieve in her culture and frequently causes her distress and shame.

Rapunzel Hohenzollen

A courageous and self-reliant young lady, Rapunzel is eager to learn about the world and fulfill her ambition, even if it means stepping outside her comfort zone.

La belle enfant

Belle has become smarter over time because she loves literature, which has improved her vocabulary, inventiveness, and open mind. She is vocal and assertive and dislikes being told what to do. Despite this, she has few friends. Her smarts and free-thinking attitude set her apart from her townmates, who find her weird despite her attractiveness.
